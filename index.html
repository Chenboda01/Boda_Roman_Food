Staple Foods

Puls (Porridge)


Puls was a thick porridge that was a staple food in ancient Rome.

Meat and Fish

Pork

The most popular meat was pork, especially sausages.

Beef was rare and expensive.

Dormice

The Romans also ate dormice, a type of rodent, which were considered a delicacy.
This glirarium unearthed in Pompeii, Italy, is a terracotta container used to house dormice.

Chicken and Fish

Chicken and fish were also popular.

Exotic Meat

Exotic meat such as giraffe and flamingo tongue for the elite.
